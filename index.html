Social Media Usage Trends

by Destiny Arlotta

Data was collected from Pew Research Center.

Facebook continues to dominate the social media market as the most highly used platform for all American age groups. 88% of people aged 18-29, and 79% of 30-49 year olds use Facebook. The sole listed social media site that stands competition is Instagram, with 59% of 18-29 year olds utilizing their platform. One may believe that Instagram's rise in usage is troublesome news to Facebook, however, Instagram is owned by Facebook and is therefore a partner not a threat.

Overall, the age group utilizing the given social media tools most often are the 18-29 year olds. Pinterest, LinkedIn, and Twitter have similar statistics, each with the younger demographic being their highest users, and having roughly 10% of 65+ users.

Data was collected from Pew Research Center.

Since 2013, social media sites have become more popular platforms for receiving news. Reddit had 62% of their site users seek news on the site in 2013. In 2016, that percentage increased to 70. Facebook's users who view news on the site increased from 47% to 66%. The trend was least influential for YouTube users, as the difference was only 1%. One of the largest increases in usage of the site for news coverage was Instagram. With a jump from 13% in 2013 to 23% in 2016, Instagram users are finding the photo site to be a source for news.
